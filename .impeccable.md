# Bakers Lounge / Neighbours Cafe Design Context

## Audience

Local St Kilda East visitors, brunch regulars, dog owners, and people checking the cafe quickly from mobile before deciding to visit.

## Visual Thesis

A bold Chapel Street cafe poster: real courtyard photography, cobalt street-art energy, amber warmth, short visitor copy, and restrained motion.

## Content Plan

- Hero: make the brand/place unmistakable, with the courtyard as the dominant visual.
- Support: show the cafe feel and street-art ambience without random photo blocks.
- Detail: highlight signature food, courtyard, dog-friendly service, and location.
- Final CTA: make hours, address, and directions instantly usable.

## Interaction Thesis

- Hero entrance and subtle image depth should create atmosphere immediately.
- Scroll reveals should clarify section rhythm, not perform for their own sake.
- Hover states should make menu, dog menu, directions, and story actions feel tactile.

## Rules

- Work in this real project: `/Users/bhavyakaushal/dev/neighbours-cafe/WEB`.
- Preserve the existing route structure and reusable components unless a change is needed.
- Use only local assets from `public/assets/cafe`.
- Keep the brand warm, local, dog-friendly, creative, and premium but approachable.
- Avoid generic cafe templates, remote stock imagery, fake graffiti, noisy sticker chaos, and corporate SaaS-style cards.
- Keep full-bleed image-led moments; do not box the hero inside a card.
- Use typography hierarchy before adding decorative elements.
- Check mobile and desktop: hero text must not overpower the photo or collide with navigation.
- Correct address language: `42 Chapel Street, St Kilda / St Kilda East VIC 3182`; mention Chapel Street and Inkerman Street when helpful.
