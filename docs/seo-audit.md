### NAP Consistency Rules

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. Every mention of the business across the web must be identical.

**Canonical NAP format (use this exactly, everywhere):**
```
Neighbours Café
42 Chapel St, St Kilda East VIC 3183
```

- The website footer, Contact/Find Us page, GBP, Facebook, Instagram bio, Yelp, TripAdvisor, Zomato, Broadsheet listing, and any directory must use this exact format
- Do not abbreviate "Street" to "St" in some places and spell it out in others — pick one format (abbreviated "St" is fine) and use it universally
- Do not list the suburb as "St Kilda" on some platforms and "St Kilda East" on others — it is St Kilda East (postcode 3183)
- Get listed on: Google Business Profile, Apple Maps (Apple Business Connect), Yelp Australia, TripAdvisor, Zomato, Broadsheet, TimeOut Melbourne, The Urban List

## 6. Quick Wins

These five actions have the highest impact-to-effort ratio and should be executed before anything else.

### Quick Win 1 — Fully optimise the Google Business Profile (Impact: Very High / Effort: Low / Timeline: 1–2 days)

The GBP is the most direct path to ranking for "dog friendly café near me," "brunch near me," and "vegan café St Kilda" without a website even being live. If the profile is incomplete or the categories are wrong, every other SEO action is undermined.

Actions:
- Set primary category to Café, add secondary categories (Breakfast restaurant, Vegan restaurant)
- Write the full 750-character description using the copy in Section 5
- Add all attributes (outdoor seating, dogs allowed, vegan options)
- Upload at minimum 20 photos: courtyard with dogs, food dishes, interior, exterior signage, team
- Seed the Q&A section with the three questions in Section 5
- Respond to every existing review within 48 hours — review velocity and response rate are GBP ranking signals

### Quick Win 2 — Build and publish the Dog Menu page as the first new page (Impact: High / Effort: Medium / Timeline: 1 week)

No competitor in St Kilda East has a dedicated, optimised Dog Menu or dog-friendly page. This is an uncontested keyword space. A single well-built page at /dog-menu targeting "dog friendly cafe St Kilda East," "dog menu cafe Melbourne," and "dog friendly outdoor cafe Melbourne" can rank in positions 1–5 within 60–90 days because the competition is thin and the intent is highly specific.

Actions:
- Build the page using the title tag, meta description, H1, keywords, and copy points from Section 4
- Add FAQ schema for the 4 dog-related questions
- Submit the URL to Google Search Console for indexing immediately after publishing

### Quick Win 3 — Get listed (or update your listing) on The Yap Pack, Puppy Tales, and Pet Parking (Impact: High / Effort: Low / Timeline: 3–5 days)

Neighbours is already mentioned on theyappack.com.au and petparking.com.au. These pages rank on page 1 for "dog friendly cafe St Kilda" and send referral traffic. The wins:
- Verify your listing is accurate on both sites (correct address: 42 Chapel St, St Kilda East — not just "St Kilda")
- Contact Puppy Tales (puppytales.com.au) to be added to their Melbourne dog-friendly café list — Neighbours is not currently listed
- Contact Urban List to be included or updated in their dog-friendly Melbourne café roundup
- Each inclusion is a high-DA backlink and a direct traffic driver

### Quick Win 4 — Fix the title tag and meta description on every page at launch (Impact: High / Effort: Low / Timeline: Same day as site launch)

Every competitor audited is missing meta descriptions or using generic title tags (e.g., "HOME | Laschicas," "Hello Sparrow | St Kilda Cafe"). This is free real estate on the SERP. Using the title tags and meta descriptions from Section 4 means Neighbours Café will have stronger click-through rates than competitors from day one — before backlinks, before reviews, before any content marketing.

Actions:
- Implement every title tag and meta description from Section 4 before the site goes live
- Do not use the CMS default (which is usually the page name only)
- Check all tags render correctly with a browser plugin or the search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly tool

### Quick Win 5 — Publish one indexable, text-based menu (Impact: Medium-High / Effort: Low / Timeline: At site launch)

Every café that uses a PDF menu or an image-based menu is invisible to Google for dish-level searches. Dish names like "Vegan Chilli Hash" and "Greens & Grains Bowl" are indexable long-tail keywords. A competitor who puts all dishes as HTML text on their menu page captures searches like "vegan chilli hash Melbourne" or "greens and grains bowl St Kilda" — these are low-volume but zero-competition queries with direct purchase intent.

Actions:
- Publish the menu as HTML text (not a PDF, not an image)
- Include dish names, short ingredient descriptions, and dietary labels (vegan, gluten-free, vegetarian) as text
- No dedicated content marketing required — this is a structural fix that pays off immediately
